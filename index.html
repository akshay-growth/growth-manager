Case Study 01
From Information to Story: How Redesigning a Product Page Almost Doubled Conversions
ICICI Prudential Life Insurance
87.5% Lift in Traffic-to-Lead 7.5% → 14% Conversion Rate UX + CRO
+
The Problem
The landing page for ICICI Prudential Life's guaranteed return plan was a wall of information , product features, policy terms, benefit tables , all structured for compliance, not for customers. Traffic-to-lead conversion sat at 7.5%. Visitors were arriving, reading, and leaving without acting. We knew the product was strong. The page wasn't doing it justice.
What We Did
  • Mobile-first redesign: Rebuilt the entire page architecture starting from the smallest screen, ensuring every element earned its space.
  • Storified structure: Replaced information-dump layout with a narrative flow , opened with a powerful headline featuring 2 core benefits on the first fold.
  • Online benefit hook: Surfaced the digital-exclusive benefit prominently in the hero fold to incentivise immediate action.
  • Persona-based recommendation: Added "Recommended for you" section matching user profiles to plan benefits.
  • How the plan works: Created illustrated step-by-step explanation with an embedded video , reducing cognitive load on complex insurance mechanics.
  • 3 simple steps to buy: Collapsed the purchase journey into a visual three-step guide.
  • Trust layer: Added "Why choose ICICI Prudential Life?" with verified trust markers (awards, claim settlement ratio, customer base).
  • FAQs from real customers: Compiled questions directly from call recordings and sales team interviews , not generic FAQs.
💡 Key Insight
Relevant information, presented in the right sequence, converts better than more information. Customers don't need to know everything , they need to understand enough to act. By moving from an information architecture to a storytelling architecture (and doubling the page length to do it), traffic-to-lead conversion grew from 7.5% to 14% , an 87.5% increase. The insight echoed across every product page we touched after this.
Case Study 02
90 Minutes to 10 Minutes: Automating Budget Reporting Across Marketing Platforms
USDC Global → MPOWER Financing
89% Time Reduction 100+ Hours Saved / Month Marketing Automation
+
The Problem
Every month, the marketing team spent 90+ minutes manually consolidating ad performance data across Google Ads, Meta, and CRM platforms into a budget report. The process was error-prone, repetitive, and left no time for analysis. The report answered "what happened" , never "what should we do next." Budget decisions were being made on stale data, often days after the fact.
What We Did
  • Mapped all data sources: Audited every platform feeding into the report , Google Ads, Meta Ads Manager, CRM exports, and manual trackers.
  • Built a unified budget consolidation sheet: Created a single master dashboard using automated API pulls and structured data templates that refreshed daily.
  • Cross-platform normalization: Standardized KPIs (spend, impressions, conversions, CAC) across platforms into a single comparable view.
  • Predictive layer: Added month-end projection formulas using pacing rates , so the team could see budget burn trajectory and course-correct mid-month.
  • Replicated at MPOWER: Adapted the same system for MPOWER Financing's global paid media operation across 9+ countries, reducing reporting time from 80 minutes to under 10.
💡 Key Insight
The real cost of manual reporting isn't the 90 minutes , it's the strategic decisions that never happen because the team is too busy building the report. By automating the consolidation layer, we unlocked time for weekly budget optimization, scenario planning, and channel rebalancing. At MPOWER, this system alone freed up ~100 hours per month across the team , time that directly contributed to the ROAS scaling work that followed.
Case Study 03
Reviving Paid Media: From Zero to 6500% ROAS Across 9+ Countries
MPOWER Financing
6500% Peak ROAS 170% YoY Revenue Growth 9+ Countries Scaled Paid Media Strategy
+
The Problem
In early 2025, MPOWER Financing paused all paid media. The intent was cost savings , but the data told a different story. HVID volumes in India fell below 2022 levels. In Ghana and Nigeria, growth stalled at 1.0x year-over-year. Competitors (Prodigy Finance, Avanse) began bidding on MPOWER's own branded keywords, capturing high-intent traffic that should have been ours. Brand search volume , a leading indicator of awareness , flatlined for the first time in three years. Organic and affiliate channels, without paid media amplification, could not compensate. The funnel was starving at the top.
What We Observed
  • MPOWER's share of India web traffic dropped by 26.38 percentage points while Prodigy (↑7.37pp) and Avanse (↑7.29pp) gained.
  • Even after pausing paid, residual conversions continued , 15% of ID Verified app submits still attributed to past paid campaigns via cookie retention (90 days) and browser auto-fill.
  • 54% of paid media impact came from awareness campaigns, not just branded search , proving the upper funnel was driving delayed conversions months later.
  • A 2024 analysis showed $7.6M in paid spend drove $75.3M in total funnel value (10% CAC) when cross-channel uplift was included.
The Strategy We Built
  • Data-backed business case: Built a comprehensive paid media investigation (presented to leadership) quantifying the cost of not advertising , lost brand share, stalled HVID volumes, competitor gains.
  • Phased pilot approach: Proposed a $150K phased pilot (Phase 1: Aug–Sep, Phase 2: Oct–Nov) targeting three market types , defend markets (India, Nigeria, Ghana), expand markets (Bangladesh, Nepal, Pakistan), and domestic markets (US, Canada).
  • AdAi integration: Deployed AdAi for branded keyword campaigns , a tool that only bids when competitors are actively bidding, reducing average CPC by 28% and saving $4K in the initial test window.
  • Signal-based targeting: Worked with iQuanti to expand application-stage tagging, feeding real-time quality signals back to Google , improving Eligibles-to-ID Verified conversion from 25% to 28%.
  • Country-level playbooks: Assigned defensive vs. offensive strategies per market based on competition intensity, organic penetration, and referral network strength.
  • Scaled to profitability: From the pilot, steadily expanded across markets, optimizing channel mix, creative, and audience targeting , ultimately reaching 6500% ROAS and contributing to 170% YoY revenue growth.
💡 Key Insight
Paid media has a long tail , and an equally long absence tail. When you stop advertising, the damage doesn't show immediately (cookies persist, direct traffic buffers the drop), but the compounding erosion shows up 2–3 quarters later across every channel. The playbook for reviving paid isn't just about turning campaigns back on , it's about making the case with data, starting with precision, and scaling only what the numbers justify. That's how a $150K pilot became a global operation running at 6500% ROAS.
